Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

The Little Prince

"What is essential is invisible to the eye."

★ ★ ★

Timeline — Chapters I to VIII

⌄
Chapter I

The Drawing

As a child, the narrator draws a boa constrictor digesting an elephant. Adults mistake it for a hat, discouraging his artistic ambitions. He grows up to become a pilot, forever aware of how "grown-ups" lack imagination.

"Grown-ups never understand anything by themselves, and it is tiresome for children to be always and forever explaining things to them."

Chapter II

Draw Me a Sheep

The pilot crashes in the Sahara Desert. A mysterious little boy appears and asks him: "Please… draw me a sheep!" After several failed attempts, the narrator draws a box, saying the sheep is inside. The prince is delighted.

"If you please — draw me a sheep!"

Chapter III

A Very Small Planet

The narrator tries to learn where the prince comes from. The little boy evades questions but reveals that his planet is very, very small — so small he needs the sheep to eat unwanted plants.

"Where I live, everything is so small…"

Chapter IV

Asteroid B-612

The narrator reveals the prince's planet is Asteroid B-612, discovered by a Turkish astronomer. Adults only believe in numbers and facts, missing the wonder. The narrator tells this story to honour the memory of his friend.

"Figures are a matter of indifference to me… I should have liked to begin this story in the fashion of the fairy-tales."

Chapter V

The Baobabs

The prince warns about baobab trees — if not pulled up early, their roots can grow so large they break a small planet apart. Discipline and daily effort are essential: "You must see to it that you pull up regularly all the baobabs."

"It is a question of discipline… You must see to it that you pull up regularly all the baobabs."

Chapter VI

Forty-Four Sunsets

The prince loves to watch sunsets. On his tiny planet, he can move his chair a few steps and watch the sun set whenever he feels sad. One particularly melancholic day, he watched forty-four sunsets.

"You know — one loves the sunset, when one is so sad…"

Chapter VII

The Thorns

The prince grows distressed, asking whether sheep eat flowers with thorns. The narrator, busy fixing his engine, dismisses the question. The prince breaks down in tears — to him, the flower is more important than anything mechanical.

"If someone loves a flower, of which just one single blossom grows in all the millions and millions of stars… it is enough to make him happy just to look at the stars."

Chapter VIII

The Rose

The prince describes his special rose — beautiful, proud, and vain. He cared for her, protected her from drafts and caterpillars, but her contradictory behaviour made him doubt her love. He decides to leave his planet, beginning his journey across the universe.

"I ought to have judged by deeds, and not by words. She cast her fragrance and her radiance over me. I ought never to have run away!"
